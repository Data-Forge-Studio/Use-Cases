# UC-BETA-022 — Multi-Day Decline Screen

**Timeframe:** 1–4 weeks

## Thesis

A quality stock experiencing three or more consecutive trading days of decline, showing oversold technical readings, with volume patterns suggesting the selling pressure is genuinely exhausting rather than accelerating into a panic. The thesis is contrarian: sustained, orderly selling in an otherwise fundamentally sound name often represents a reversal opportunity once the selling pressure is spent, distinct from a genuine deterioration in the underlying business.

## Entry Signal

Three or more consecutive down days, an oversold reading on daily momentum indicators, and a declining volume pattern through the decline — orderly distribution rather than the volume spike characteristic of panic or capitulation selling. The underlying fundamental thesis for the stock must remain genuinely intact, and the broader macro backdrop should not be in a severe, systemic bear phase that would override an individual stock's technical setup.

## Exit Rules

The profit target is a recovery back toward the price level that existed before the decline began. The stop is triggered by a fresh 52-week low, or by a sudden spike in volume on continued weakness — which would indicate the orderly decline has shifted into genuine capitulation that has not yet completed.

## A Note on Repeated Signals

A stock appearing repeatedly in this screen over consecutive days or weeks indicates it remains in a sustained downtrend that has not yet reversed. This reflects the screen correctly identifying ongoing weakness, not a malfunction — the reversal simply has not occurred yet, and may not occur at all if the underlying decline reflects a genuine change in fundamentals rather than a temporary overreaction.
