# UC-BETA-006 — Wheel Strategy

**Timeframe:** Ongoing income

## Thesis

A systematic, two-phase options income strategy. In the first phase, cash-secured puts are sold on quality stocks at a strike price below the current market price — a level at which the underlying stock would genuinely be wanted as a holding if assigned. If assigned, the second phase begins: covered calls are sold against the newly acquired shares, generating ongoing premium income while effectively lowering the cost basis over time. The wheel describes the cyclical nature of rotating between these two option-selling phases.

## Entry Signal

Phase one entry requires a strike price 5–10% below the current market price, positioned near a meaningful technical support level, with an expiration window of 30 to 45 days and a minimum premium target proportional to the strike value. Critically, the underlying stock must be one that is genuinely wanted as a long-term holding if assignment occurs — this strategy should never be applied to a stock that is not independently a quality candidate, since assignment is a real, expected outcome of the strategy, not an edge case.

## Exit Rules

If assigned in phase one, covered calls begin immediately at phase two: a strike roughly 5% above the assignment price or the nearest technical resistance level, again with a 30-to-45-day expiration. Calls are never written through a known earnings date, since the added volatility distorts the premium-to-risk relationship. If the underlying stock experiences a genuine fundamental deterioration at any point in the cycle, the wheel is stopped — continuing to write calls on a fundamentally broken stock compounds the original loss rather than recovering it.
