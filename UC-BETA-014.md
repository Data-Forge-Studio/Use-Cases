# UC-BETA-014 — Insider + Analyst + Price Confluence

**Timeframe:** Varies

## Thesis

No single data point is, on its own, a reliable trading signal. This use case requires three genuinely independent signals to align in the same direction simultaneously: insider buying, analyst sentiment improvement, and a confirmed price breakout. The thesis is that when three unrelated sources of information — company insiders with privileged knowledge, professional analysts with sector expertise, and the aggregate judgment of the broader market reflected in price — all independently point the same direction, the combined signal carries meaningfully more weight than any single component alone.

## Entry Signal

Genuine insider buying (not merely option exercises, which are a weaker, more ambiguous signal) within the prior 30 days, combined with at least one analyst upgrading their rating to a buy-equivalent recommendation, combined with a confirmed technical price breakout. All three legs are required — removing any single one materially weakens the conviction behind the setup and the position does not qualify for this use case.

## Exit Rules

Exit rules follow the underlying technical setup that triggered entry (swing or medium-hold logic, depending on the nature of the breakout). Critically, the position is exited immediately if any of the three original legs reverses — particularly if insiders begin selling after the position was entered, which is treated as a meaningful change in the original thesis regardless of price action.
