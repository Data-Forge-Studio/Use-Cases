# UC-BETA-009 — Post-Earnings Overreaction Recovery

**Timeframe:** 1–6 weeks

## Thesis

Markets sometimes overreact to earnings reports that are not actually as negative as the initial price reaction suggests — a one-time charge, a conservative guidance reset, or a headline miss that obscures otherwise solid underlying performance. The thesis is to identify cases where the market's emotional, immediate reaction has overshot the genuine fundamental impact, and to buy the overreaction with the expectation that price recovers as the market reassesses the report more carefully in the following days.

## Entry Signal

The stock must be down meaningfully (5–15%) on the earnings reaction, but the underlying long-term fundamental thesis must remain genuinely intact — this is not a bet on every earnings dip recovering, only ones where the selloff appears disconnected from a real change in the business outlook. There should be no guidance cut that meaningfully alters the longer-term trajectory. Technical indicators should show the stock in oversold territory, with selling volume beginning to fade rather than accelerating.

## Exit Rules

The profit target is a recovery back toward the pre-earnings price level. The stop is triggered if the decline continues meaningfully past the initial reaction, which would suggest the overreaction thesis was simply wrong and the market's initial assessment was closer to correct. A four-week time stop applies if the anticipated recovery has not materialized.
