# UC-BETA-015 — TSX Energy Royalty Income

**Timeframe:** Indefinite

## Thesis

Toronto Stock Exchange-listed energy royalty and pipeline infrastructure companies characteristically offer high, relatively stable cash distributions, often structured to pass through a significant share of operating cash flow to unitholders. The thesis treats these as long-term income-generating holdings rather than growth positions, valued primarily for the durability and size of their distribution rather than capital appreciation potential.

## Entry Signal

Distribution yield above 5%, with a payout ratio assessed as sustainable relative to underlying cash flow generation rather than financed through incremental debt. A reasonably strong balance sheet, and a broader commodity macro backdrop that is not in a deep, structural bear phase that would threaten the underlying cash flows supporting the distribution.

## Exit Rules

Exit is triggered by a genuine distribution cut, which signals real financial stress in the underlying business rather than a temporary commodity price fluctuation. A structural change to the royalty or partnership arrangement itself, or a severe, sustained deterioration in the relevant commodity macro environment, also warrants exit consideration.
