# UC-BETA-007 — US Large Cap Growth (Long Term)

**Timeframe:** Indefinite

## Thesis

Allocating to established United States large-capitalization growth companies as long-term holdings, recognizing that certain sectors and business models — particularly in technology and innovation-driven industries — have historically been more concentrated in US markets than in Canada. The thesis is the same long-term compounding logic applied to blue chip holdings, extended to capture growth exposure not as readily available domestically.

## Entry Signal

Positions are built through systematic monthly contributions rather than attempting to time entries. Meaningful market dislocations — declines of 15% or more from recent highs — are treated as opportunities to accelerate contributions beyond the regular schedule, since broad market corrections in quality growth names have historically represented attractive long-term entry points rather than signals to wait on the sidelines.

## Exit Rules

No price target is set for these holdings — the strategy is to compound through market cycles. Periodic rebalancing may trim positions that have grown to represent an outsized share of the overall portfolio, but full exits are reserved for genuine, structural changes to a company's competitive position or for capital reallocation needs unrelated to the position's own performance.
