# UC-BETA-010 — Post-Earnings Continuation (Buy the Beat)

**Timeframe:** 1–4 weeks

## Thesis

When a company beats earnings expectations and raises forward guidance, and the stock responds with a genuine, sustained price gap higher, the thesis is that institutional buyers are likely still in the process of accumulating the position rather than having already completed their buying. Strong reactions to strong results, particularly when guidance is also raised, frequently see follow-through in the days and weeks after the initial reaction as the broader market continues to digest the improved outlook.

## Entry Signal

The earnings report must show a genuine beat accompanied by raised forward guidance — a beat without a guidance raise is treated as a meaningfully weaker signal. The stock should be up at least 5% on the earnings day itself and should hold those gains into the following session rather than fading, which would suggest the initial enthusiasm was not durable. Volume should remain elevated, and momentum indicators should be rising without already being deeply overbought.

## Exit Rules

The profit target is set 10–15% above the post-earnings price level. The stop is triggered if price falls back into the pre-earnings trading range, which would indicate that institutional buyers were not, in fact, continuing to accumulate and the initial pop has been fully absorbed or reversed.
