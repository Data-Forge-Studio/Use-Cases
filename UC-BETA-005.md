# UC-BETA-005 — Income Strategy (Dividend + Covered Call)

**Timeframe:** Indefinite

## Thesis

Holding quality, dividend-paying TSX equities while systematically selling covered call options against the position to generate an enhanced, combined income stream from both the dividend and the option premium. The thesis treats the underlying equity as a long-term holding first and the option-writing as a supplementary income overlay, not a separate speculative activity — the stock would be held regardless of whether calls are being written against it.

## Entry Signal

Underlying stock selection follows the same quality criteria as the core long-term holdings: dividend yield above 3%, sustainable payout ratio, strong balance sheet. Covered calls are written only when implied volatility is elevated enough (above roughly the 30th percentile of its own historical range) to justify the premium received relative to the opportunity cost of potentially capping upside.

## Exit Rules

The position itself is held indefinitely, consistent with its underlying long-term thesis. Being assigned and having shares called away at the strike price is treated as an acceptable, planned outcome — effectively realizing a sale at a predetermined, attractive price — not a failure of the strategy. Calls are typically written 15 to 30 days to expiration, at strikes 5–10% above the current price.
