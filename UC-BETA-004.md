# UC-BETA-004 — Earnings Play (Pre-Earnings Momentum)

**Timeframe:** 2–4 weeks

## Thesis

Entering a position ahead of a scheduled earnings announcement when technical momentum, analyst sentiment, and insider trading activity all independently align in the same direction. The thesis is built on the well-documented pre-earnings drift phenomenon — stocks with strong setups heading into earnings often continue to drift in that direction in the days leading up to the announcement, separate from the earnings result itself, as anticipatory positioning builds. This is explicitly not a bet on the earnings outcome.

## Entry Signal

The earnings date should fall within a two-to-four week window from entry. Analyst sentiment ahead of the report should be net bullish, with no recent insider selling in the preceding 30 days (insider selling ahead of earnings is treated as a meaningful red flag regardless of how positive other signals appear). The technical setup should show either a confirmed breakout or the stock holding firmly above a key support level heading into the report.

## Exit Rules

The position is explicitly closed before the earnings announcement itself — this use case captures the pre-earnings drift, not the earnings outcome, which is a fundamentally different and far less predictable bet. Holding through the report converts a probabilistic momentum trade into a binary gamble on a single data point, which is outside the intended risk profile of this strategy.
