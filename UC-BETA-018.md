# UC-BETA-018 — Covered Call Candidate Scanner

**Timeframe:** Ongoing

## Thesis

A systematic scan of existing holdings and watchlist names for stocks whose options currently carry elevated implied volatility — meaning option premiums are richer than their historical norm — making them attractive candidates for covered call writing. The thesis is purely about yield enhancement on positions already held or genuinely wanted: when implied volatility is elevated, the premium received for selling upside potential is more favorable relative to the opportunity cost.

## Entry Signal

Implied volatility rank at or above the 40th percentile of the stock's own historical range, no earnings announcement scheduled within the next 45 days (earnings-driven volatility spikes distort the premium calculation), and either an existing holding of at least 100 shares or a genuine willingness to acquire shares specifically to write calls against them.

## Exit Rules

Calls are closed for a profit once 50% of the maximum potential premium has been captured, rather than holding to expiration and risking a late reversal. If the short call position is tested (price approaching the strike) with less than seven days to expiration, the position is rolled forward to a later date and often a higher strike rather than allowing assignment to occur reactively.
