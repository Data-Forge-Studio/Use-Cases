# UC-ALPHA-004 — Whale Wallet Follow (Accumulation)

**Timeframe:** 1–3 months

## Thesis

On-chain blockchain data is public and auditable, which means large wallet behavior can be observed directly rather than inferred. When sustained, deliberate accumulation by large holders is visible in a quality asset, the thesis is that sophisticated, well-capitalized participants are positioning ahead of an anticipated move — whether driven by a known catalyst, private research, or simply a longer time horizon than the broader retail market is willing to wait for.

## Entry Signal

Increasing concentration of supply in large wallets over a sustained period, typically two weeks or more, rather than a single large transaction (which is more often noise or an exchange-internal transfer than a genuine signal). Elevated outflows from centralized exchanges into self-custody wallets, which historically correlates with longer-term holding intent rather than near-term selling. Price action during this accumulation phase typically shows compression — a tightening trading range — rather than the volatility associated with active distribution.

## Exit Rules

Exit is triggered by the appearance of distribution signals: the same large wallets that were accumulating begin moving balances back toward exchanges, or concentration metrics reverse and begin declining. A loss of price momentum without a clear catalyst materializing is also treated as a signal that the accumulation thesis may not play out within the expected window.

## What This Is Not

On-chain data shows what large wallets are doing, not why. This is directional evidence, not certainty — large holders can be wrong, can be positioning for reasons unrelated to near-term price (such as long-term treasury management), or can reverse their own positioning without warning.
