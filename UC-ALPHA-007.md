# UC-ALPHA-007 — Large Cap Medium Hold (3–6 months)

**Timeframe:** 3–6 months

## Thesis

A position scaled between the short-term swing trades and the indefinite long-term holds: a quality large-cap crypto asset with a specific, identifiable catalyst expected to materialize within a defined three-to-six month window. Examples of qualifying catalysts include network protocol upgrades with confirmed timelines, regulatory decisions with scheduled review dates, or structural supply events. The thesis is to accumulate ahead of the catalyst, tolerate interim volatility, and capture the price move once the catalyst resolves — rather than trying to time entry around the event itself.

## Entry Signal

A specific, dated catalyst must be identifiable with a reasonably defined timeline — vague optimism about 'eventual adoption' does not qualify. The asset should be in an accumulation or basing phase ahead of the catalyst rather than already extended in price. The expected risk-to-reward profile, measured from current price to a realistic target if the catalyst materializes favorably, should be at minimum 3-to-1.

## Exit Rules

The position is closed when either the catalyst plays out (regardless of whether the outcome was favorable or unfavorable — the trade thesis is resolved either way) or when the expected timeline passes without the catalyst materializing. Holding indefinitely past the original catalyst window without a fresh thesis is explicitly avoided — this prevents a time-bound trade from quietly becoming an unplanned long-term hold.
