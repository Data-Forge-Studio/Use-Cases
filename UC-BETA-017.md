# UC-BETA-017 — TSX Insider Cluster Buy

**Timeframe:** Varies

## Thesis

Multiple insiders at the same company independently purchasing shares within a short window, with no offsetting insider selling, is treated as one of the strongest available signals in equity analysis. The thesis is that cluster buying is rarely coordinated for appearance's sake — when several individuals with direct knowledge of the business independently choose to commit personal capital around the same time, it reflects a genuine, shared conviction about the company's near-term prospects that is difficult to fabricate or game.

## Entry Signal

At least three distinct insiders purchasing shares within a 30-day window, with zero insider selling during the same period (any offsetting selling meaningfully weakens the signal and may disqualify the setup entirely). A genuine technical setup should also be present — insider buying alone, without a corroborating price or volume pattern, is treated as a weaker standalone signal.

## Exit Rules

Standard swing-trade exit logic applies based on the technical setup that accompanied the insider activity. Any insider selling that occurs after entry is treated as an immediate, significant red flag warranting prompt exit, regardless of where price currently stands relative to entry.
